Title: #insight

群體智慧有個很苛刻的前提： 一群人必須各自獨立做判斷，彼此之間不受影響。

現實的問題是 大多數決策根本不是獨立做出來的。 人會互相影響，會觀察別人怎麼想，會根據群體反應調整自己的判斷。 當一個群體裡的每個人都在參考別人，而不是直接面對事實時，所謂的“群體智慧”就會變成“群體失真”。

模仿是人性裡非常深的一部分。 文化夜市靠模仿在社會裡傳遞。 但問題是，很多錯誤，偏見和荒謬的共識，也同樣是通過模仿被擴散和放大的。

如果你想做真正偉大的事，就必須有能力逆着人群走。 他們厲害的地方，不是更擅長追隨共識，而是更能承受自己和大多數人不一樣。

Title: #softskills

How to have a hard conversation - Remember showing up imperfectly is better than not showing up at all + avoiding the hard conversations ensures comfort. Confronting them invites growth, which do you value more?

- Define key terms being discussed in the hard conversation.

    +  If your partner isn’t texting you enough … what does ‘enough’ mean to you?
         If it’s regarding your performance at work, how does your boss define ‘productivity’?
         Often our conflicts are fuelled by miscommunication more than anything else.

- Replace judgement with curiosity.
    + Curiosity turns an argument into a conversation.
        Instead of rushing to make your point, urge for more clarification.
        Replace rebuttals with, “I want to fully understand. Can you explain that to me a bit more?”

- Allow room for silence. Even if it’s uncomfortable.
    + Silence to process is better than speaking just to speak.
       This also gives the other person the opportunity to fly finish their thought.
